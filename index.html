Psychotherapy

Andrea offers a compassionate, direct approach to individual therapy. Her role is to support you in reaching toward your best self, the one you may have lost touch with. She is an active-listener, supportive and non-judgmental. She will provide an alternate perspective and help you discover the patterns getting in your way.

Andrea feels strongly about utilizing evidence-based practices and regularly incorporates cognitive behavioral therapy and mindfulness meditation into her work. She is trained in EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing), a specialized treatment that is particularly effectively for trauma survivors. Additionally, Andrea’s therapy practice is grounded in feminist an relational theories. Her focus on social justice and trauma-informed care are critical components of her therapy work.

Andrea has extensive training and knowledge working with anxiety, depression, PTSD, relationship difficulties and substance use concerns. She has a deep commitment to working with individuals identifying within LGBTQ+ communities. Andrea has a dedication and a familiarity in affirmatively practicing with individuals identifying as kinky, poly and folks describing their relationship structures as open or non-monogamous. She regularly serves as a support for folks exploring sexuality and gender identity.

Individual therapy is highly personalized and tailored to meet your specific needs. Andrea provides short term services for immediate concerns and longer-term therapy for chronic issues. Please remember that sessions are confidential and any information we exchange is considered protected health information.

Clinical Supervision & Consultation

Over the past several years, Andrea has cultivated a passion for supporting new clinicians and building strong clinical teams. She has had the opportunity to supervise graduate students, licensed social workers and other master’s level clinicians working toward their full clinical licensure. Additionally, she has provided individual supervision to more senior clinicians — licensed clinical social workers, licensed clinical professional counselors and licensed clinical psychologists. Andrea sees clinical supervision as a collaborative, relational process. Discussions of challenging clinical interactions are paired with an intentional focus on the impact of the clinical work on the clinician.

Bio

Andrea is an experienced psychotherapist and clinical supervisor with over ten years of post-graduate experience. She has trained and worked in an outpatient clinic specializing in the unique needs of LGBTQ clients and HIV impacted communities. She has provided therapy and advocacy in a program tailored to those who have experienced intimate partner violence. Andrea has completed psychiatrric evaluations in emergency rooms, worked in outpatient clinics, community mental health centers and private therapy practices.

Andrea has worked in specialized outpatient and intensive outpatient substance use programs, utilizing both abstinence-based and harm reduction frameworks. Much of her work has focused on supporting clients in their exploration of trauma. She received her Bachelor of Arts in Psychology and Women’s Studies as well as a Master of Social Work with a concentration in mental health, both from the University of Wisconsin.
